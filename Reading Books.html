Books Worth Reading Again

There are many other good books, but start with these.

The Rudest Book Ever

A refreshing, easy-to-read, and relatable guide, will make you rethink everything you’ve been taught. This book gives you the practical approach of your life.

But be warned: the author has no desire to spare your feelings. What you will find in this straight-forward, straight-talking, no-craps-given guide.


How to Win Friends and Influence People

How to Win Friends and Influence People teaches you how to communicate properly amd behave with others.

Read it multiple times to remind yourself the basic principles of communication


The Power Of Habit

The book delves into matters like why habits exist and how they can be changed for a better way of business and eventually life.

It explores the science behind habit creation and reformation.


Ikigai

Ikigai explains how you can live a longer and happier life by having a purpose, eating healthy, and not retiring.

Ikigai is a Japanese concept referring to something that gives a person a sense of purpose, a reason for living.
This book will help you work out what your own ikigai really is, and equip you to change your life.


Rich Dad Poor Dad

This book focuses on financial literacy, financial independence, building wealth by investing in financial assets and increasing one’s financial intelligence.

This book shows how we, the majority of the people think, & that's why we remain poor all our life.
